7 Benefits Of Being A Freelancer.

02ndApril 2018

By Haydn Hadley

A freelancer is a person who is self-employed and offering their services, most commonly to businesses and often to multiple clients at a time. The type of work freelancers do varies. Every service a business could need can be provided by a freelancer, including, digital marketing, such as social media marketing, technological support, such as web programming and design, copywriting, and publicity, writing, such as articles and blog posts, and creative works such as graphic design. Freelancing is so flexible, I can set my own hours, working full or part-time on the projects of my choosing. While clients can offer specifications to the work, I work similarly to an independent contractor, in which I'm free to control how the work is completed. Also, freelancing allows me to set my own price, which is higher than what I'd make as an employee doing the same work. The workload does vary. Many freelancers build up a client base over a period of time. For example, I have a client that requires a podcast to be transcribed twice a week so that he can repurpose the podcast into blog posts, this massively increases his SEO. Others work with clients over shorter periods, usually on single projects. For example, a freelance web designer might build a website for a client and once the site is done, so is their working relationship.

There's a number of perks to working as a freelancer, including:

I got started quickly

I already knew the skill I would offer, getting started was simply a matter of finding my first client. Which meant...

It was easy to start

If you have a skill you can start right now too, using your network find a client. While you'll want to build a LinkedIn profile and/or a website, you can network within the parameters of your current career and friend networks to find your first client. It won't harm you to join a freelancer platform like PeoplePerHour also.

It's Affordable

So if you have the ability to provide the service, chances are you also have whatever equipment or software you need to deliver it. All I needed to get started in freelancing as an audio transcriptionist was a laptop. Eventually, I'd like to invest in business building tools, such as growing this website, but using LinkedIn (which is free) is a great online resume that can help me promote my service.

There's a high demand for help

Yes, the marketplace of freelancers is competitive, the need for quality, reliable freelancers is growing. A lot of businesses don't have employees instead, they have a team of freelancers.

I choose my own schedule

I work when and where I want. Just last week my girlfriend had a business trip in the Cotswolds, I didn't require permission from a boss, neither did I use up any valuable holiday days that I used to save up. I just went. I spent three days working in little cafes (my office is where ever they serve good coffee, it's my only rule).

I pick and choose my clients

While I'm still in the beginning stages of my freelance career (the bulk of my work comes from jobs I take on via an online transcription company), I take any client that will hire me, as I grow, I will choose not to take on difficult clients, or difficult jobs with poor audio quality.

I do the work the way I see fit

While I strive to deliver what the client asks, how the work is done, ultimately, is up to me.
